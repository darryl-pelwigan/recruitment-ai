# 4. Introduction

Recruitment AI is an online platform that connects Applicants with job opportunities and helps Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators manage hiring from a single place.

Depending on your role, Recruitment AI gives you different tools:

- **Applicants** can browse Job Postings, apply, track their Applications, manage their Resumes, and save jobs for later.
- **Recruiters** and **HR staff** can post and manage Job Postings, review Applicants, and move them through a hiring Pipeline.
- **Administrators** can do everything Recruiters and HR staff can do, and also manage user accounts across the platform.

This manual explains how to use every page in Recruitment AI, organized by the tasks you are most likely to perform.

# 5. System Requirements

You can use Recruitment AI from a desktop computer, tablet, or mobile phone, using a modern web browser. An internet connection is required. No additional software needs to be installed.

# 6. Creating an Account and Logging In

## Register

### Overview

Before you can use Recruitment AI, you need an account. The Register page lets a new user create one. An account is required to apply for Job Postings, track Applications, or manage hiring, depending on your role.

The Register page is shown below.

![Register](screenshots/register.png)

What you can do on this page:

- Enter your Full Name, Email, and Password
- Choose your role: Applicant or Recruiter
- Select "Create Account" to finish
- Select "Sign in" if you already have an account

### Procedure

1. Open Recruitment AI and select "Sign up" from the Login page.
2. Enter your Full Name, Email, and a Password.
3. Choose whether you are registering as an Applicant or a Recruiter.
4. Select "Create Account".
5. You are signed in and taken to your Dashboard.

### Notes

- Administrator and HR accounts cannot be created from this page. They are assigned manually by an existing Administrator through User Management.
- If you already have an account, select "Sign in" instead of registering again.

### Tips

- Choose the role that matches what you plan to do in Recruitment AI: select Applicant if you want to apply for jobs, or Recruiter if you plan to post and manage Job Postings.
- Use a password you have not used elsewhere, since it protects access to your Applications or hiring data.

## Login

### Overview

The Login page is where you sign in to Recruitment AI using your email address and password. Signing in confirms who you are and shows you the version of Recruitment AI suited to your role.

The Login page is shown below.

![Login](screenshots/login.png)

What you can do on this page:

- Enter your Email and Password
- Select "Login" to sign in
- Select "Sign up" to go to the Register page if you do not have an account

### Procedure

1. Open Recruitment AI. If you are not already signed in, you are shown the Login page.
2. Enter the email address associated with your account.
3. Enter your password.
4. Select "Login".
5. You are taken to your Dashboard once your details are confirmed.

### Notes

- If your email or password is incorrect, you will see an error message and can try again.
- There is no self-service "forgot password" option on this page. If you cannot remember your password, ask your Administrator to reset it from User Management.

### Tips

- Keep your password somewhere safe. You can change it later from your Profile page.
- If you are using a shared computer, remember to log out when you are finished.

# 7. Dashboard

## Overview

The Dashboard is the first page you see after signing in. It gives you a quick summary of what is happening in Recruitment AI, tailored to your role, so you do not have to visit several pages just to see how things are going.

The Dashboard is shown below.

![Dashboard](screenshots/dashboard.png)

For Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators, the Dashboard shows:

- A welcome message with your name and role
- Key numbers: Active Jobs, Total Applicants, Hired, and Conversion Rate
- A list of Applicants per Job Posting, showing how many people have applied to each one
- A summary of the Top Skills found across your Applicant pool
- Quick-action shortcuts to Post a Job and View All Applicants

For Applicants, the Dashboard shows:

- A welcome message with your name
- A summary of your Applications, broken down by status (Applied, Under Review, Shortlisted, Interview Scheduled, Rejected, Hired)
- Quick-action shortcuts to Browse Jobs, My Applications, My Resumes, and Saved Jobs

## Procedure

1. Sign in to Recruitment AI. You are taken to the Dashboard automatically.
2. Review the summary numbers at the top of the page to understand current activity at a glance.
3. Select any job title in the "Applicants per Job" list to open that Job Posting's Applicants page directly.
4. Select a quick-action card, such as "Post a Job" or "Browse Jobs", to jump straight to that task.

## Notes

- The information shown depends on your role. Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators see company-wide hiring data. Applicants see only their own activity.
- If you have not posted any Job Postings or submitted any Applications yet, some summary numbers will show as zero.

## Tips

- Use the Dashboard as your starting point each time you sign in, so you always know what needs your attention before digging into individual pages.
- Recruiters can jump straight to a busy Job Posting's Applicants page from the "Applicants per Job" list instead of searching for it on the Jobs page.

# 8. Browsing and Posting Jobs

## Jobs

### Overview

The Jobs page lists Job Postings available in Recruitment AI. What you see and can do here depends on your role. The page heading changes depending on who is viewing it: Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators see "My Job Listings" or "All Job Listings", while guests and Applicants see "Explore Jobs".

The Jobs page is shown below.

![Jobs](screenshots/jobs.png)

For guests and Applicants:

- Browse all open Job Postings
- Search by title or description
- Filter by Job Type, Location, and Posted date

For Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators:

- Toggle between "Mine" (Job Postings you created) and "All" Job Postings
- Additional filters for Status and Applicant Stage
- "Post Job" button to create a new Job Posting
- "Edit", "Close", and "Delete" actions on each Job Posting
- A count of Applicants per Job Posting, linking directly to that job's Applicants page

Long lists of Job Postings are split across pages. Use the page numbers, or the "Previous" and "Next" buttons, at the bottom of the list to move between them.

### Procedure

1. Open the Jobs page from the main navigation bar.
2. Use the search box to look for a specific role, or use the filters to narrow the list by type, location, or other criteria.
3. If the list spans more than one page, use the page numbers or the "Previous" and "Next" buttons to see more results.
4. Select a Job Title to view its full details.
5. If you manage Job Postings, use "Mine" or "All" to switch between your own postings and every posting in the system.
6. Select "Post Job" to create a new listing, or use "Edit", "Close", or "Delete" on an existing listing to manage it.

### Notes

- Applicants and guests only see Open Job Postings.
- The "Mine" view for Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators shows only Job Postings they personally created.

### Tips

- Applicants should check back regularly or search by keyword, since new Job Postings are added over time.
- Recruiters should close a Job Posting as soon as it is filled to keep the "All" list accurate for the whole team.

## Job Details

### Overview

The Job Details page shows the full description of a single Job Posting. It can be viewed by guests, Applicants, Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators, though what you can see and do depends on whether you are signed in and what role you have.

The Job Details page is shown below.

![Job Details](screenshots/job-detail.png)

What this page shows:

- Company name, Job Title, Status, Employment Type, Location, and posting date
- Full Job Description and Requirements
- List of required skills
- A Job Overview panel with type, location, date posted, salary, and contact email
- "Apply Now" for signed-in Applicants
- "View Applicants", "Pipeline", and "Edit Job" actions for Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators

### Procedure

1. Select a Job Posting title from the Jobs page to open its details.
2. Review the description, requirements, and skills to understand the role.
3. If you are a signed-in Applicant and want to apply, select "Apply Now" and follow the on-screen prompts.
4. If you are a Recruiter, HR staff member, or Administrator, use "View Applicants", "Pipeline", or "Edit Job" to manage the posting.

### Notes

- Guests who are not signed in can see most job information, but not the salary range, and cannot use "Apply Now". Sign in as an Applicant to see the salary and apply.
- Only Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators see the management actions ("View Applicants", "Pipeline", "Edit Job").

### Tips

- Read the full Requirements list before applying, so you can tailor your Application to the role.
- Recruiters can use this page as a quick checkpoint before sharing a Job Posting link externally.

## Post a Job (Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators only)

### Overview

The Post a Job page lets Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators create a new Job Posting for Applicants to discover and apply to. A clear, complete Job Posting attracts the right Applicants and sets expectations before anyone applies.

The Post a Job page is shown below.

![Post a Job](screenshots/post-job.png)

What you can fill in:

- Job Title, Location, Employment Type, and Status
- Salary range with currency selection
- Skills Required
- Company Info, including company name, contact email, and logo
- A rich text Job Description
- A Requirements list

### Procedure

1. Select "Post Job" from the Jobs page or from your Dashboard.
2. Enter the Job Title and Location.
3. Choose the Employment Type and set the Status to "Open" so Applicants can see it.
4. Enter the salary range and select the currency.
5. List the required skills, separated by commas.
6. Fill in the Company Info section, including a contact email Applicants can reach, and select "Upload Logo" if you want to add a company logo.
7. Write the Job Description using the formatting toolbar, and list the Requirements.
8. Select "Post Job" to publish the listing.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.
- A Job Posting with Status set to "Closed" will not appear to Applicants browsing the Jobs page.

### Tips

- Be specific in the Requirements section. Clear requirements help Applicants self-select and save you time reviewing Applications.
- Double-check the contact email before publishing, since this is how Applicants may reach out with questions.

## Edit Job (Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators only)

### Overview

The Edit Job page lets Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators update the details of a Job Posting that has already been created, without recreating it from scratch.

The Edit Job page is shown below.

![Edit Job](screenshots/edit-job.png)

This page uses the same fields as Post a Job, pre-filled with the Job Posting's current details.

### Procedure

1. Open the Job Posting you want to change and select "Edit Job".
2. Update any fields that need to change, such as the salary range or Status.
3. Use the formatting toolbar to adjust the Job Description if needed.
4. Review the Requirements list and update it if the role has changed.
5. Select "Save Changes" to update the Job Posting, or "Cancel" to return without saving.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.
- Setting Status to "Closed" hides the Job Posting from new Applicants but keeps existing Applications visible.

### Tips

- Close a Job Posting as soon as the role is filled, so Applicants do not apply to a position that is no longer available.
- Double-check the salary range and currency before saving, since these are some of the first details Applicants see.

# 9. Applying to Jobs and Tracking Applications

## Applying to a Job (Applicants only)

### Overview

Once you find a Job Posting you are interested in, you can apply directly from its Job Details page. See [Job Details](#job-details) in the previous chapter for everything else that page shows.

### Procedure

1. Open the Job Posting you want to apply to.
2. Review the description, requirements, and skills.
3. Select "Apply Now".
4. Follow the on-screen prompts to submit your Application.

### Notes

- You must be signed in as an Applicant to see "Apply Now".
- Once submitted, your Application appears on the My Applications page.

### Tips

- Make sure your Resume is up to date before applying, since it is used automatically when you submit an Application. See the [Resumes](#12-resumes) chapter for details.

## My Applications

### Overview

My Applications shows every Job Posting you have applied to, along with the current status of each one, so you can keep track of your job search in one place.

The My Applications page is shown below.

![My Applications](screenshots/my-applications.png)

What this page shows:

- A list of every Job Posting you have applied to
- The current status of each Application (Applied, Under Review, Shortlisted, Interview Scheduled, Rejected, Hired)
- A link to browse open positions if you have not applied to any Job Postings yet

### Procedure

1. Select "My Applications" from the navigation bar or from your Dashboard.
2. Review the list of Job Postings you have applied to and their current status.
3. Select a Job Posting to view its details again if you want to review the description or requirements.
4. If you have not applied to anything yet, select "Browse open positions" to find a role.

### Notes

- This page is available to Applicants only.
- Statuses are updated by the Recruiter, HR staff member, or Administrator managing that Job Posting, so a status change may not appear immediately after an interview or decision.

### Tips

- Check back periodically, since Recruiters update statuses as your Applications move through the Pipeline.
- If an Application has stayed on "Applied" for a long time, consider applying to similar roles while you wait.

# 10. Managing Applicants and the Pipeline

This chapter is for Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.

## Applicants

### Overview

The Applicants page gives you a single place to browse every Applicant who has applied across all of your Job Postings, instead of checking each Job Posting one at a time.

The Applicants page is shown below.

![Applicants](screenshots/all-applicants.png)

What you can do on this page:

- Search Applicants by name, email, or headline
- Filter Applicants by how recently they were active
- Filter by skill using the skill tag buttons
- Bookmark an Applicant directly from their card
- Open an Applicant's full profile with "View Profile"
- Open an Applicant's Resume, when one has been uploaded

### Procedure

1. Open the Applicants page from the main navigation bar.
2. Type a name, email, or headline into the search box to narrow the list, then select "Search".
3. Use the "Active Within" filter to focus on Applicants who have used the platform recently.
4. Select a skill tag to show only Applicants who list that skill.
5. Select the bookmark icon on an Applicant's card to save them for later, or "View Profile" to see their full details.
6. Select "Resume" on an Applicant's card to open their uploaded Resume, if one is available.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators. Applicants do not see this page.
- If no Applicants match your search or filters, the page will tell you so you can adjust your criteria.

### Tips

- Bookmark strong Applicants as soon as you find them, even if you are not ready to move them into a Pipeline yet. You can find them again from Saved Applicants.
- Combine the skill filter with a search term to narrow large Applicant pools quickly.

## Applicants for a Job Posting

### Overview

Each Job Posting has its own Applicants page, listing everyone who has applied specifically to that role, so you can review and manage them without looking through your entire Applicant pool.

The Applicants page for a single Job Posting is shown below.

![Job Applicants](screenshots/applicants.png)

What you can do on this page:

- View the list of Applicants who applied to this Job Posting, with their email and application date
- See each Applicant's AI match score, showing how closely their profile matches the Job Posting
- Read each Applicant's cover message
- Change an Applicant's status using the dropdown next to their name (Applied, Under Review, Shortlisted, Interview Scheduled, Rejected, Hired)
- Filter the list by status
- Select "Re-score All" to refresh the AI match scores for every Applicant
- Open the Pipeline view for this Job Posting

### Procedure

1. Open a Job Posting and select "View Applicants", or select an Applicant count from the Dashboard.
2. Review each Applicant's match score and cover message.
3. Use the status dropdown next to an Applicant to move them to the next stage, such as "Shortlisted" or "Interview Scheduled".
4. Use the "Filter" option to show only Applicants at a particular stage.
5. Select "Re-score All" if you have updated the Job Posting and want fresh match scores.
6. Select "Pipeline" to see all Applicants for this Job Posting organized by stage on a board.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.
- The AI match score is a guide to help you prioritize review. It does not replace reading the Applicant's full profile.

### Tips

- Update an Applicant's status as soon as you make a decision, so your Pipeline stays accurate for everyone on your team.
- Use the status filter to quickly find Applicants still waiting on "Applied" or "Under Review".

## Applicant Profile

### Overview

The Applicant Profile page shows the full details of a single Applicant, including their contact information, experience, skills, and Resume, so you can evaluate them in depth before deciding whether to move them forward for a Job Posting.

The Applicant Profile page is shown below.

![Applicant Profile](screenshots/applicant-profile.png)

What this page shows:

- The Applicant's name, headline, email, phone number, and location
- A link to download or view the Applicant's Resume
- Years of experience and expected salary
- Links to the Applicant's LinkedIn and portfolio, when provided
- The Applicant's skills
- A professional summary written by the Applicant
- A "Bookmark" button to save the Applicant for later

### Procedure

1. From the Applicants page or a Job Posting's Applicants list, select an Applicant's name or "View Profile".
2. Review their contact details, experience, and skills.
3. Select "Resume" to open their uploaded Resume in a new tab.
4. Select "Bookmark" if you want to save this Applicant to your Saved Applicants list.
5. Select "Back" to return to the previous page.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.
- Some fields, such as LinkedIn or portfolio links, only appear if the Applicant provided them.

### Tips

- Check the Resume link before contacting an Applicant, since it often contains details not shown on the profile itself.
- Use the Bookmark button here instead of navigating back to the Applicants list, to save time.

## Pipeline

### Overview

The Pipeline page shows all Applicants for a single Job Posting organized into stages on a board, so you can see hiring progress at a glance and move Applicants forward without leaving the page.

The Pipeline page is shown below.

![Pipeline](screenshots/pipeline.png)

What this page shows:

- Columns for each stage: Applied, Under Review, Shortlisted, Interview Scheduled, Rejected, and Hired
- A count of Applicants in each column
- Drag-and-drop cards to move an Applicant from one stage to another

### Procedure

1. Open a Job Posting's Applicants page and select "Pipeline".
2. Review how many Applicants are in each stage.
3. Drag an Applicant's card from one column and drop it into another column to update their stage.
4. Select "Back to Applicants" to return to the list view.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.
- Moving a card updates the Applicant's status immediately, and the same change is reflected on the standard Applicants list.

### Tips

- Use the Pipeline view during hiring meetings so everyone can see progress on a role at a glance.
- Keep the "Applied" column moving regularly so new Applicants are not overlooked.

# 11. Saved Jobs and Saved Applicants

## Saved Jobs (Applicants only)

### Overview

Saved Jobs lists every Job Posting you have bookmarked to apply to later, so you do not have to search for it again.

The Saved Jobs page is shown below.

![Saved Jobs](screenshots/saved-jobs.png)

What this page shows:

- Every Job Posting you have bookmarked, with its title, company, Status, Employment Type, Location, and salary range
- The date you saved the job
- "View Job" to open the full Job Details page
- "Remove" to un-bookmark a job

### Procedure

1. While browsing the Jobs page, select "Save job" on a listing you are interested in.
2. Select "Saved Jobs" from your account menu to see everything you have bookmarked.
3. Select "View Job" to read the full details and apply when you are ready.
4. Select "Remove" if you are no longer interested in a saved job.

### Notes

- This page is available to Applicants only.
- If you have not saved any jobs yet, this page will tell you and suggest browsing the Jobs page.

### Tips

- Save a job as soon as you find it interesting, even if you plan to finish your Application later.
- Check your Saved Jobs before a Job Posting closes, since a saved job can still be filled by other Applicants.

## Saved Applicants (Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators only)

### Overview

Saved Applicants, shown in the app as "Bookmarked Applicants", lists every Applicant you have bookmarked for future reference. Not every strong Applicant fits your current openings, so bookmarking lets you keep track of promising people to reach out to when a matching Job Posting becomes available.

The Saved Applicants page is shown below.

![Saved Applicants](screenshots/saved-applicants.png)

What this page shows:

- Every Applicant you have bookmarked, with their headline, contact email, location, experience, and skills
- A link to their Resume, when available
- "View Profile" to open their full Applicant Profile
- "Remove" to un-bookmark an Applicant

### Procedure

1. Bookmark an Applicant from the Applicants page or their Applicant Profile by selecting the bookmark icon or "Bookmark" button.
2. Select "Bookmarked Applicants" from your account menu to see everyone you have saved.
3. Select "View Profile" to review an Applicant again, or "Resume" to open their Resume.
4. Select "Remove" if an Applicant is no longer relevant to keep on this list.

### Notes

- This page is available to Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators.
- If you have not bookmarked anyone yet, this page will tell you and suggest bookmarking Applicants from their profile page.

### Tips

- Review your Saved Applicants whenever you post a new Job Posting, since a previously bookmarked Applicant may be a great fit.
- Remove Applicants who are no longer looking for work to keep this list useful.

# 12. Resumes

## Overview

Resumes, shown in the app as "Resume Storage", is where you, as an Applicant, manage the Resume files linked to your account and to individual Applications. Recruiters review your Resume when deciding how to move your Application forward, so keeping it current and accessible matters.

The Resumes page is shown below.

![Resumes](screenshots/resumes.png)

What this page shows:

- Your Profile Resume, which is automatically linked whenever you apply for a Job Posting
- "Preview", "Download", and "Replace" actions for your Profile Resume
- A list of Application Resumes, showing the Job Title, company, application date, and current status for each one
- "Preview" and "Download" actions for each Application Resume

## Procedure

1. Select "Resumes" from the navigation bar or from your Dashboard.
2. Select "Preview" to view your Profile Resume, or "Download" to save a copy.
3. Select "Replace" to upload a new version of your Profile Resume.
4. Scroll to "Application Resumes" to see the Resume you submitted for each specific Job Posting, and use "Preview" or "Download" as needed.

## Notes

- This page is available to Applicants only.
- Replacing your Profile Resume does not change the Resume already submitted for Applications you have already sent.

## Tips

- Keep your Profile Resume up to date, since it is used automatically the next time you apply for a Job Posting.
- If you tailor your Resume for a specific role, check the "Application Resumes" list to confirm the right version was submitted.

# 14. User Management (Administrators Only)

## Overview

User Management gives Administrators a complete list of every account in Recruitment AI, along with the ability to change roles and reset passwords. As the platform grows, Administrators need a way to see who has access, correct a role that was set up incorrectly, and help users who are locked out of their accounts.

The User Management page is shown below.

![User Management](screenshots/user-management.png)

What this page shows:

- A total count of users in the system
- Search by name or email
- Filter by role (Applicant, Recruiter, HR, Administrator)

## Procedure

1. Select "User Management" from your account menu.
2. Use the search box or role filter to find a specific user.

## Notes

- This page is available to Administrators only.
- You cannot change your own role from this page. Your own account is shown for reference but without a role dropdown.

## Tips

- Double-check a user's identity before changing their role, since roles control what pages and actions they can access.
- Use the role filter to periodically review how many Recruiters, HR staff, or Administrators currently have access.

# 15. Frequently Asked Questions

**What roles exist in Recruitment AI?**
There are four roles: Applicant, Recruiter, HR, and Administrator. You can register as an Applicant or a Recruiter. HR and Administrator accounts are assigned manually by an Administrator through User Management.

**I forgot my password. What do I do?**
The Login page does not have a self-service password reset option. Ask your Administrator to reset your password from User Management.

**Why can't I see a job's salary?**
Salary details are only shown to signed-in Applicants. If you are browsing as a guest, sign in or create an account to see the full Job Details, including salary.

**How do I know if a Recruiter has reviewed my Application?**
Check the My Applications page. Your Application's status changes as a Recruiter, HR staff member, or Administrator reviews it, for example from "Applied" to "Under Review" or "Shortlisted".

**Can I change my email address or password?**
Yes. Go to your Profile page to update your name, email, or password.

# 16. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| I can't log in | Your email or password is incorrect | Double-check your email and password, then try again. If you still cannot sign in, contact your Administrator. |
| I don't see a "Post Job" button | Your account role is Applicant | Only Recruiters, HR staff, and Administrators can post jobs. Contact your Administrator if you believe your role is incorrect. |
| A page sends me back to the Dashboard or Jobs page | The page is restricted to a different role | Some pages, such as Resumes and Saved Jobs, are only available to Applicants. Others, such as User Management, are only available to Administrators. |
| I can't see a job's salary | You are not signed in | Sign in to your account to see full Job Details, including salary. |

# 17. Logging Out

## Overview

When you are finished using Recruitment AI, you should log out, especially if you are using a shared or public computer.

## Procedure

1. Select your name or avatar in the top navigation bar.
2. Select "Logout" from the menu.
3. You are returned to the Login page.

## Notes

- Logging out ends your current session. You will need to sign in again to access your account.

## Tips

- Always log out when using a shared or public computer to protect your account.
